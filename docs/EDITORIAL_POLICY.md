# Editorial and Legal-Update Policy

## Source hierarchy

1. Current SRA SQE1 and SQE2 Assessment Specifications
2. SRA Statement of Solicitor Competence and published performance indicators
3. Primary legislation, rules and binding authorities
4. Authoritative procedural and regulator guidance
5. Secondary commentary used only to aid explanation

Official sample questions are used to understand format and assessment level. They are not copied into the app.

## Content version

The current curriculum structure targets the SRA specifications applicable from 1 September 2025 through 31 August 2026. Changes published for assessments from 1 September 2026 must be incorporated and re-reviewed before claiming coverage for that window.

## Required review before public commercial release

Every lesson, answer, explanation, calculation and scenario must be reviewed by a solicitor of England and Wales or a subject-matter expert operating under documented legal-editor supervision. Review records should contain:

- content ID and version;
- reviewer name and qualification;
- sources and law cut-off date;
- review date and outcome;
- amendments made;
- next scheduled review.

## Question quality

Each single-best-answer question should have a scenario stem, a clear lead-in, five plausible options, one unambiguously best answer and an explanation addressing both the correct answer and material distractors. Generated structural questions are a foundation bank and require item-by-item legal/editorial validation before high-stakes claims.

## Update cadence

- Quarterly legal-change scan
- Mandatory review after each SRA annual specification update
- Immediate correction process for material errors
- Visible content-version notice in the app
